Here's a 10-day detailed itinerary for Italy, combining scenic lakes, cultural highlights, relaxation, and adventure. Activities include pricing, meals, and experiences that highlight Italy's charm.



Day 1: Arrival in Dhaka



Morning:

  • First day in Dhaka (average nightly rates: BDT 3,500–BDT 8,000 for mid-range hotels)
  • Breakfast: Enjoy local Bangladeshi breakfast items such as paratha with egg curry or fuchka (BDT 100–BDT 300 per person)
  • After breakfast, take a leisurely stroll around Ramna Park, a peaceful urban green space where you can enjoy the morning breeze and relax by the large pond.

Afternoon:

  • Visit Visit Lalbagh Fort, a 17th-century Mughal fort complex in the heart of Dhaka. Explore its historical architecture, lush gardens, and museum.
  • Entry fee:(BDT 20–BDT 100), depending on the visitor’s nationality
  • Next, head over to Ahsan Manzil, the Pink Palace, which once served as the official residential palace of the Nawab of Dhaka. The museum inside showcases the rich history of the region.
  • Afterward, you can explore the vibrant Sadarghat River Port, one of the busiest river ports in the world. Take a boat ride on the Buriganga River to see the bustling life of the city from the water.

Dinner:

  • Head to Bismillah Hotel for classic Kacchi Biryani, a traditional Dhaka-style dish(BDT 400–BDT 600 per person)
  • If you're in the mood for something sweet, finish off your meal with a traditional dessert like Roshogolla or Mishti Doi (sweetened yogurt).


Day 2: Sunderbans National park in Khulna



Morning:

  • Take a train or bus from Dhaka to Khulna (approx. BDT 300–BDT 1,000 one-way, depending on the service).
  • Rent a boat for a Sundarbans river safari to explore the UNESCO-listed mangrove forest and enjoy views of the iconic wildlife, including Bengal tigers, saltwater crocodiles, and a variety of birds (BDT 3,000–BDT 6,000 per boat for a half-day tour).
  • Entry to Sundarbans Reserve: Entry fees to the park and designated areas can range from BDT 50–BDT 500, depending on the location and the type of tour you select.

Lunch:

  • Enjoy a traditional Bengali seafood lunch at a riverside restaurant or local eatery, with specialties like Hilsa Fish Curry, Prawn Bhorta, and Shorshe Ilish (fish cooked with mustard sauce) (BDT 400–BDT 800 per person).
  • Panta Ilish (fermented rice with fish) is also a must-try if you are exploring the local cuisine.

Afternoon:

  • Continue your boat ride along the peaceful rivers, and visit Karamjal Wildlife Sanctuary to witness the conservation efforts and see various animals like crocodiles, spotted deer, and more. You can also visit local fishing villages and interact with fishermen (entrance fee: BDT 100–BDT 200).
  • Walk along the riverbanks, observe the biodiversity of the region, and enjoy a relaxing afternoon in the heart of nature.

Dinner:

  • For dinner, head to a local restaurant in Khulna, such as Hotel Aponjon or Renaissance Restaurant, serving Bengali Macher Jhol (fish curry) and Bhuna Khichuri (rice and lentils with spices). Pair it with Mishti Doi (sweet yogurt) for dessert (BDT 350–BDT 700 per person).
  • If you’re in the mood for something unique, try a traditional Shorshe Bata Maach (fish cooked with mustard paste), which is a regional specialty.


Day 3: Sylhet



Morning:

  • Travel from Khulna to Sylhet (approx. 5-6 hours by car or bus, or a domestic flight for around BDT 3,000–BDT 5,000).
  • Arrive in Sylhet, known for its tea gardens, rolling hills, and natural beauty.
  • Breakfast: Start your day with a hearty local breakfast of Paratha, Lassi, and Chotpoti (BDT 150–BDT 300 per person) at a traditional Sylheti restaurant.

Lunch:

  • Visit a local restaurant or a tea estate café for a delightful Bengali meal. Try Panta Ilish (fermented rice with fish), Chingri Bhorta (shrimp paste), and Macher Jhol (fish curry), all paired with freshly brewed Sylheti tea (BDT 350–BDT 600 per person).

Afternoon:

  • Explore Jaflong, a scenic area famous for its hills, tea gardens, and river valleys. You can take a boat ride on the Madhabkunda Waterfall, the tallest waterfall in Bangladesh (boat ride: BDT 500–BDT 1,000).

Dinner:

  • For dinner, visit Jolsha Restaurant or Sagar Resto, where you can enjoy traditional Sylheti delicacies such as Bengali-style Fish Curry, Bhuna Khichuri, or Shorshe Ilish (BDT 400–BDT 700 per person).


Day 4: Noakhali



Morning:

  • Travel from Sylhet to Noakhali (approx. 5-6 hours by car or bus; alternatively, take a domestic flight to Chittagong, then a short drive to Noakhali).
  • Arrive in Noakhali, known for its vibrant culture, rivers, and coastal beauty.
  • Breakfast: Start your day with a local breakfast of luchi and aloo bhaji (fried flatbread with spiced potato curry) at a local restaurant (BDT 100–BDT 200 per person).

Afternoon:

  • Stop by Chandraghona, a place famous for its river-based dining experiences, and try fresh river fish, bhuna gosht, or a mix of traditional coastal seafood like prawns and crabs (BDT 300–BDT 600 per person).

Dinner:

  • Enjoy dinner at Noakhali's local eateries, such as Khulna Diner or Bongo Bhuiyan, where you can savor hearty Bengali-style dishes like Panta Bhat with Ilish Bhorta (fermented rice with mashed hilsa fish) or Prawn Curry with Rice (BDT 350–BDT 500 per person).Enjoy dinner at Noakhali's local eateries, such as Khulna Diner or Bongo Bhuiyan, where you can savor hearty Bengali-style dishes like Panta Bhat with Ilish Bhorta (fermented rice with mashed hilsa fish) or Prawn Curry with Rice (BDT 350–BDT 500 per person).


Day 5: Chittagong(Known as Chattogram



Morning:

  • Travel from Noakhali to Chittagong (approx. 3-4 hours by car or bus).Arrive in Chittagong, a bustling port city known for its coastal beauty and diverse culture.
  • Breakfast: Start your day with Chittagong’s famous kacchi Bhuna (spiced meat) served with Paratha or Ruti (BDT 150–BDT 250 per person) at a local restaurant or street food stall.

Lunch:

  • Chittagong is famous for Sea-foods.For lunch, try the local seafood at the restaurant near Joy’s Lake, where you can enjoy dishes like Chingri Bhorta (mashed shrimp), Macher Jhol (fish curry), or Chingri Malai Curry (prawn curry in coconut milk) (BDT 400–BDT 700 per person).

Afternoon:

  • After lunch, explore Kaptai Lake, which is about an hour’s drive from the city. You can enjoy a boat ride on this stunning lake surrounded by hills, and explore Rangamati, a serene town nearby known for its tribal culture and beautiful landscapes. You can also visit the Hanging Bridge in Rangamati (BDT 300–BDT 500 for boat rides).

Dinner:

  • Dinner: Enjoy a hearty meal at one of the popular restaurants in Chittagong, such as Mezban Restaurant (famous for its Mezban Beef), which is a traditional Chittagong dish (BDT 400–BDT 600 per person).
