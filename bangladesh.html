Here's a 10-day detailed itinerary for Italy, combining scenic lakes, cultural highlights, relaxation, and adventure. Activities include pricing, meals, and experiences that highlight Italy's charm.



Day 1: Arrival in Dhaka



Morning:

  • First day in Milan (average nightly rates: €80–€200 for mid-range hotels)
  • Breakfast: Enjoy Italian pastries and espresso at Pasticceria Marchesi (€5–€10 per person)

Afternoon:

  • Visit QC Terme Milano, a luxury spa in the heart of the city. Relax in the storm room, saunas, and hydrotherapy pools
  • Entry fee: (€44–€54), depending on the time and day

Dinner:

  • Head to Trattoria Milanese for classic risotto alla milanese (€20–€30 per person)


Day 2: Lake Como & Star Wars Villa



Morning:

  • Take a train to Lake Como (approx. €10 one-way)
  • Rent a boat to explore the lake (€70–€120/hour) and enjoy views of iconic villas, including Villa del Balbianello, featured in Star Wars and James Bond
  • Entry to the villa: €11 for gardens, €23 for a full guided tour

Lunch:

  • Try La Darsena in Tremezzo, serving lakeside Italian cuisine (€20–€35 per meal)

Afternoon:

  • Walk through Bellagio, the "Pearl of Lake Como," for shopping and gelato (€3–€5)

Dinner:

  • Dine at Ristorante Mistral (Michelin-starred, multi-course meals from €150)


Day 3: Sylhet



Morning:

  • Drive or take a train to Lago di Braies (5 hours from Como; car rental from €50/day)
  • Rent a traditional rowboat to explore the emerald waters (approx. €18 for 30 minutes)

Lunch:

  • Visit Hotel Lago di Braies Restaurant for local Tyrolean dishes (€25–€40 per person)

Afternoon:

  • Hike around the lake and take in the dramatic mountain views

Dinner:

  • Try a rustic dinner at Baita Valbona, known for its alpine cuisine (€20–€30)


Day 4: Lake Sorapis Hike



Morning:

  • Lake Sorapis Hike (6–7 km round trip)
  • Entry is free (but guided tours cost around €70–€100 per person)
  • Carry packed snacks or grab something light from Cortina d'Ampezzo

Afternoon:

  • Relax by the lake, famous for its vivid turquoise color

Dinner:

  • Enjoy hearty Italian fare at II Meloncino al Caminetto (€30–€50 per person)


Day 5: Sardinia - Cagliari Underground Tour



Morning:

  • Fly to Cagliari, Sardinia (flights from €50–€120). Check into a local hotel (€70–€150 per night)
  • Start the Cagliari Underground Walking Tour to explore hidden tunnels and WWII shelters (approx. €35 per person)

Lunch:

  • Try Ristorante Antica Cagliari for Sardinian pasta and seafood (€20–€35)

Afternoon:

  • Relax at Poetto Beach or snorkel along the coast (gear rental from €15)

Dinner:

  • Visit Sa Domu Sarda for authentic Sardinian lamb dishes (€25–€40)


Day 6: Devil’s Saddle Boat Tour



Morning:

  • Book a Devil’s Saddle Boat Tour:
  • Includes snorkeling at four stops and drinks
  • Approx. (€50–€70 per person)

Lunch:

  • Enjoy fresh seafood at La Marinella (€20–€30 per person)

Afternoon:

  • Kayak with dolphins near the Devil’s Saddle area (€60 per person for a guided tour)

Dinner:

  • End your day at Trattoria Lillicu for Sardinian specialties (€20–€30)


Day 7: Blue Grotto in Capri



Morning:

  • Fly to Naples and ferry to Capri (ferry tickets: €20–€25 each way)
  • Tour the Blue Grotto, a magical experience where the moon reflects in the blue sea
  • Entry: €14; boat tours around the island (from €20–€50)

Lunch:

  • Stop at Da Paolino for lunch under lemon trees (€50–€70)

Afternoon:

  • Stroll through Anacapri and take the chairlift to Monte Solaro (€12 round trip)

Dinner:

  • Dine at Ristorante La Palette for views and Mediterranean dishes (€40–€60)


Day 8: Saturnia Hot Springs



Morning:

  • Saturnia Hot Springs in Tuscany
  • Entry is free; nearby spa resorts offer day passes from (€30–€70)
  • Relax in the thermal pools

Lunch:

  • Enjoy a rustic meal at Trattoria il Rifugio (€20–€30 per person)

Afternoon:

  • Visit Pitigliano, a nearby town known as the "Little Jerusalem"

Dinner:

  • homemade pasta at Hosteria del Ceccottino (€25–€40)


Day 9: Bagni San Filippo



Morning:

  • Explore the Bagni San Filippo hot springs, known for their white limestone formations
  • Entry is free; guided tours are available (for around €50)

Lunch:

  • Picnic by the springs with items from a local market

Afternoon:

  • Stroll through Val d’Orcia, a UNESCO World Heritage Site

Dinner:

  • Enjoy Tuscan wine and steaks at Osteria del Teatro (€35–€60)


Day 10: Rome & Departure



Morning:

  • Travel to Rome and explore The Colosseum, Trevi Fountain, and Piazza Navona (combined tickets from €18–€24)
  • Breakfast: Indulge in tiramisu pancakes at Two Sizes (€5–€10)

Lunch:

  • Grab pizza al taglio (by the slice) from Pizzarium (€5–€8)

Afternoon:

  • Shop for souvenirs at Campo de' Fiori or Via del Corso before heading to the airport
