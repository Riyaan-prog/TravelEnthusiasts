Experience the magic of Morocco: 10 days of activities including quad biking, visiting markets/riads, camel rides ensuring you have an unforgettable experience.



Day 1: Arrival in Marrakech & Explore the Souks



Morning:

  • Check into a traditional Moroccan Riad. These stunning, palace-like accommodations often include ornate courtyards and MUST TRY traditional Moroccan mint tea upon arrival.(£60–£200 per night)
  • Get a beautiful breath of culture when visiting the souks (markets) with beautiful art,pottery,textiles you can pick up treasures starting at just £2

Lunch:

  • Grab lunch at Nomad, a rooftop view for modern Moroccan cuisine (£10–£15)

Afternoon

  • Visit Koutoubia Mosque, the largest mosque in Marrakech for the beautiful architecture of the place especially at sunset
  • End the day with a relaxing Moroccan spa hammam experience, a must experience. (£30–£70)


Dinner:

  • Dine at Comptoir Darna for fusion dishes and traditional yet lively entertainment (£15–£30). After dinner, head upstairs as it transforms into a chic lounge


Day 2: Camel Ride & Dinner



Morning:

  • Sunset camel ride across the Moroccan desert. Many packages include a traditional BBQ dinner with Moroccan tea and cultural performances (£45 per person)

Lunch:

  • Return to the city for lunch at La Trattoria, an elegant Italian-Moroccan restaurant known for its comfortable garden setting (£20–£35)

Afternoon:

  • Relax at Le Jardin Majorelle, a stunning garden designed by Yves Saint Laurent (entry: £10)

Dinner:

  • End your day with traditional Moroccan food at Dar Moha, trying local specialties (£30–£50)


Day 3: Paradise Valley & Crocodile Park



Morning:

  • Paradise Valley, an ethereal experience with natural pools and cliffs perfect for a refreshing dip (£18 per person for a tour). Pack light snacks for the hike

Lunch:

  • Stop at a local café in the valley offering tagines and fresh juices for around £8–£12

Afternoon:

  • Visit Crocodile Park in Agadir, view over 300 crocodiles and exotic plants (£22 entry)


Dinner:

  • Return to Agadir for dinner at a beachside restaurant like Pure Passion, serving fresh seafood and Moroccan wines (£20–£35)


Day 4: Adventure Day - Quad Biking & Go-Karting



Morning:

  • Quad biking in the Moroccan countryside. Involves sand dunning and a great glimpse of moroccan culture. Packages include lunch(from £25 per person)

Afternoon:

  • Go-karting on water at the N.A.R Complexe Sportif near Marrakech (approx. £25 per person)


Dinner:

  • Dine at Bacha Coffee, a luxurious spot offering over 200 coffee blends alongside pastries and light meals (£10–£20)


Day 5: Ouzoud Waterfalls



Full-Day Excursion:

  • Embark on a 10-hour day trip to the stunning Ouzoud Waterfalls, one of Morocco’s most famous natural sites. You can swim, hike, or spot wild monkeys in the area (£15 per person)

Lunch:

  • Eat at a riverside café serving fresh tagines for around £8

Dinner:

  • Return to Marrakech andhave dinner at Al Fassia, run entirely by women and known for its slow-cooked lamb dishes (£20–£35)


Day 6: Essaouira Beach



Morning:

  • Drive to the breezy Essaouira Beach (approx. 2.5 hours from Marrakech)

Lunch:

  • Savor fresh seafood at Restaurant La Chalet de la Plage (£15–£25)

Afternoon:

  • Try sandboarding along the dunes (£25 per person) or ride horses on the beach at sunset for just £5

Dinner:

  • Treat yourself to a seafood platter at The Loft (£20–£40)


Day 7: Agafay Desert Adventure



Morning:

  • Head to the Agafay Desert, just outside Marrakech. Activities include camel rides, tours, and luxury lunch under the desert sky (£25-£100)

Lunch:

  • A picnic in the desert, often included in tour packages

Afternoon:

  • Relax in the calm desert ambiance, then return to Marrakech

Dinner:

  • Indulge in fusion cuisine at Le Foundouk (£20–£40)


Day 8: Pottery & Local Markets



Morning:

  • Take a pottery workshop in Marrakech to create your own art (£25 per person)

Lunch:

  • Visit Café des Épices for a simple and delightful Moroccan lunch on a rooftop terrace (£8–£15)

Afternoon:

  • Spend time shopping for souvenirs, ruhs, spices,clothes etc in the souks or markets from £2

Dinner:

  • Head to Azar (pictured above) for a mix of Moroccan and Mediterranean dishes served with a modern twist (£20–£35)


Day 9: Cultural Exploration



Morning:

  • Visit the Ben Youssef Madrasa, a beautiful islamic school with intricate carvings (entry: £6)
  • Stop by the Bacha Coffee Museum, learning about coffee culture whilst tasting samples(£8 for a coffee tasting)

Lunch:

  • Enjoy lunch at Riad Dar Attajmil, has a beautiful garden view (£15–£25)

Afternoon:

  • Relax with a traditional hammam spa treatment to rejuvenate (£30–£70)

Dinner:

  • Opt for dinner at the trendy La Mamounia Hotel, one of Morocco’s most iconic spots (£50+)


Day 10: Departure



Morning:

  • Spend your last day taking in Kasbah Oulad Othmane Zagora, showcasing Morocco’s history. Guided tours cost around £15
  • Stop for original Moroccan coffee or a quick pastry before heading to the airport
